## Code of Conduct

1. We permit and encourage the reasonable use of AI or LLMs as auxiliary tools, including but not limited to:
   a. Literature retrieval and organization;
   b. Code assistance and debugging suggestions;
   c. Language polishing and expression improvement.
2. Academic ideas, research questions, research design, technical roadmaps, experimental schemes, core conclusions, and innovation points must be completed and fully understood by the student/advisor. AI / LLMs may only provide auxiliary support; the lead role in research or key academic judgments must not be delegated to AI tools.
3. For any content generated or assisted by AI / LLMs, the student must verify its accuracy item by item, and ensure it is fully consistent with the actual research process, experimental results, and facts. The use of AI tools must not introduce errors, exaggerations, inconsistencies, or descriptions that cannot be reproduced.
4. Do not use AI / LLMs to generate, fabricate, or guess academic literature, reference entries, or citation information. All cited references must come from real, verifiable sources, and must be retrieved, read, and confirmed by the student personally.
5. Do not use AI / LLMs for any form of academic misconduct, including but not limited to:
   a. Generating fake data, experimental results, or citations;
   b. Fabricating, tampering with, or making up experimental processes;
   c. Concealing plagiarism of others' work or improper paraphrasing.
6. In academic paper submissions, dissertation writing, or other formal academic outputs, if journals, conferences, or schools have explicit disclosure or restriction requirements regarding the use of AI, they must be strictly followed; in case of uncertainty, consult the advisor in advance.

## Background

### A Revolution Sparked by a Tweet

In February 2025, former Tesla AI Director and OpenAI co-founder Andrej Karpathy posted a tweet on social media:

> There's a new kind of coding I call "vibe coding," where you fully give in to the vibes, embrace exponentials, and forget that the code even exists.

The reason this tweet sparked widespread discussion is that — we are entering an era where we can "make" software without having to "write" code.

### "Vibe Coding" Becomes the Word of the Year

In November 2025, Collins Dictionary announced: "Vibe Coding" was selected as the Word of the Year.

The official definition of this term is:

> A way of generating computer code using artificial intelligence through natural language descriptions.

In other words, you don't need to learn any programming language. Just use natural language to tell the AI, "I want an XX," and it will build it for you.

### The Data Doesn't Lie

**Y Combinator's Astonishing Finding**

In March 2025, Garry Tan, CEO of the world's most famous startup incubator Y Combinator, revealed:

In the latest batch of startups, 25% of the companies reported that 95% of their code was generated by AI.

These are not casual hobby projects, but real startups that are actively fundraising and growing. Even more surprisingly, **these companies typically have teams of fewer than 10 people**, building products that previously required dozens of engineers to complete.

## Vibe Coding

> The core shift of Vibe Coding: from "writing code" to "expressing clearly + judging right from wrong"

### When We Go from Coder to Commander

**What has changed?**

| Before | Now |
|------|------|
| Memorize syntax rules | Clearly describe requirements |
| Write code by hand | Judge whether the code is correct |
| Debug and find bugs | Describe the problem and let the AI fix it |
| Read technical documentation | Know what to ask and what to look up |

**What hasn't changed?**
- You need to know what you want: no matter how smart the AI is, it cannot figure out what to do for you
- You need to judge whether the result is good or not: the AI can make mistakes, and you must be able to spot them
- You need to have problem-solving thinking: when issues arise, know how to troubleshoot step by step

### To Do a Good Job, First Sharpen Your "Tools"

In 2025, AI coding tools are as plentiful as food-delivery apps. For Researchers, we recommend using the most cutting-edge "models" + the most usable "tools."

## Methodology (Mindset, Techniques)

> We help everyone master the methodology of Vibe Coding from two complementary angles — "mindset" and "techniques" — to write higher-quality code with higher efficiency.

### Mindset (Principles to Keep in Mind)

- **Whatever the AI can do, do not do it manually**
- **Ask the AI any question**
- **Goal-driven**: all actions during development revolve around the "goal"
- **Context is the first-principle element of Vibe Coding (Context is King)**: "Garbage in, Garbage out"
- **Occam's Razor**: if not necessary, do not write unrelated code — reduce interference from irrelevant context
- **Subtractive thinking (MVP development)**: with the minimum input (Minimum), verify core hypotheses (Visible), and deliver the smallest unit of value (Product)

### Techniques (Tips You Can Leverage)

**1. Plan First, Execute Later**

For Cursor, when implementing a minimal MVP, use Plan Mode (toggle with Shift+Tab) to let the AI generate a plan first. Once verified correct, switch to Agent Mode.
- Ensure the AI understands the Idea you want to implement, the Implementation, and the Goal of the implementation
- Detect any misunderstanding by the AI early and correct it promptly

**2. Explicit Problem Requirements**

> **Explicit requirements = less rework + fewer errors**

- What do you want to implement?
- What is the function?
- What are the I/O inputs and outputs?
- Special coding conventions, additional requirements, or constraints? For example, do not use LangChain for construction — use the native OpenAI package to call the LLM; apply parallel optimization whenever possible; etc.

> **Don't make the AI guess what you want; tell the AI what you want to do and how it should be done**

```
I want to implement a functional module [Module Name]. The purpose of this module is [Function Description].
Its input is [Input], and its output is [Output].

Notes:
- The input of the module is a JSON file, where each Object has the structure {"key": value, ...}
- The output of the module is a JSON file, and I can configure the save path of the output file; the output format is {"key": value, ...}
- The module must have detailed Logging; you may use the Loguru Python Package
- Use multi-threading / multi-processing parallelism to optimize execution efficiency, and I can configure the degree of parallelism
```

**3. Cost (Small Quick Steps) <<< Cost (Big Leap Forward)**

<ol type="a">
  <li>Each time, let the AI implement a Minimum Functional Unit</li>
  <li>Upon completion, immediately conduct Code Review / Testing</li>
  <li><strong>‼Human supervision is still important‼</strong>
    <ol type="i">
      <li>Avoid code becoming the last line of defense of a pile of garbage!</li>
      <li>You do not have to read every line of code carefully, but you need to make sure the overall logic is what you want (matches expectations)</li>
      <li>Prevent accumulating large amounts of unreviewed code that deviates from expectations</li>
    </ol>
  </li>
  <li>Remember: steady small-step implementations always beat an all-at-once big leap forward</li>
</ol>

**4. Manage Context (Context is King)**

<ol type="a">
  <li>The model easily triggers Context Limit within a single Chat Session (reaching maximum context)
    <ol type="i">
      <li>Tools like Cursor or Claude Code will perform Context Summarization</li>
      <li><strong>However, this does not mean information can be preserved losslessly!</strong></li>
      <li>Therefore, complex tasks can be split into multiple Chat Sessions</li>
    </ol>
  </li>
  <li>When you find the AI has "forgotten" the "conventions" you required earlier, or has hallucinated, etc.:
    <ol type="i">
      <li>Immediately supplement the context related to the current requirement (see Point 2 "Explicit Problem Requirements")</li>
      <li>It is recommended to open a new Chat Session to keep the model's Attention on the current requirement</li>
    </ol>
  </li>
</ol>

**5. When Correcting Errors, Provide Sufficient Error Information**

When the AI-generated code throws an error, provide enough error context:

- Which file contains the error?
- What is the run command?
- What error occurred? (Error Stack Traceback in the Terminal)

Example:

```text
Wrong way to ask:
My code has an error when running, please help me fix it.

Correct way to ask:
My Python script crashes when computing the total price. Please help me fix it based on the following context and error stack.

1. The file with the error (shopping_cart.py)
2. Run command: `python shopping_cart.py`
3. Terminal error message (Error Stack Traceback):
Traceback (most recent call last):
File "shopping_cart.py", line 8, in <module>
total += item['price'] * item['qty']
TypeError: can't multiply sequence by non-int of type 'float'
```

**6. (Advanced) Version Control**

a. Practice / become proficient with Git Add/Commit/Push commands
```bash
git add <files>
git commit -m "Detailed Comments"
```

b. Every time a minimum functional unit is completed, let the AI perform a Git Commit with detailed Comments, for example:
```
refactor: clean up unused code and optimize app structure
- Remove unused imports and redundant functions across `app/`.
- Extract hardcoded BIRD dataset mappings to `app/db_utils/constants.py`.
- Clean up `__init__.py` exports and fix typos in `app/db_utils/schema.py`.
- Update default SQL execution timeout in `app/config/config.py`.

Co-authored-by: Cursor <cursoragent@cursor.com>
```

c. Good version control benefits the entire project development cycle
- The AI breaks originally correct code → roll back the version
- Multi-branch development → team collaboration with fewer conflicts

## Vibe Figure

> For a paper (Research Project), the end of Vibe Coding marks the completion of "Experiments."

Next, we enter the writing of the Paper, starting first with Figures — such as the Teaser Figure, Overview Figure, Motivation Figure, etc.

### Step 1: Figure Design

The core logic of this step is to clearly explain the "paper logic," then discuss with the LLM the figure details / your expectations.

- Based on my logic, design the main figure of my paper. First propose several possible drawing styles, and after discussion we decide on which form to use.
- Based on the chosen form, generate a Prompt that can be used directly for Gemini image generation. Require all text in the figure to be in English, the font to be [specified], and the quality to meet [target conference] standards, 8K.

### Step 2: Draft Figure Generation

Use a powerful text-to-image model to generate a draft figure. We recommend Gemini on the web / Nano Banana Pro. Input the drawing prompt obtained in Step 1, and iterate the design rapidly.

### Step 3: Redraw as a Vector Figure

Draft figures generated by Gemini / Nano Banana Pro are raster images (PNG) and cannot be directly used in the Paper.

We need to use drawing tools to manually redraw vector figures (PDF / SVG) based on the draft.

- Web-based Figma: https://www.figma.com
- MacOS OmniGraffle
- PowerPoint (PPT)

There is no hierarchy of quality among drawing tools — whichever one you are proficient with is best.

## Vibe Writing

### Writing Guidelines

- **Do not directly copy Paper content generated by AI / LLMs**. Students must verify the accuracy of content item by item, ensuring full consistency with the actual research process, experimental results, and facts.
- **Do not let AI / LLMs write paper references, guess references, or freely cite references, etc.**
- AI / LLMs may only provide auxiliary support; the leading role of research or key academic judgments must not be delegated to AI tools.

### Techniques

- **Context is King — provide comprehensive, detailed context**
  - Research question, research background, existing work, existing limitations, core challenges, proposed method
  - Experimental data tables (in LaTeX format)
  - Paper figures (upload using multimodal models such as Gemini / GPT)
- **LLMs are limited to assisting writing ideas; students should independently understand, verify, and write the Paper content**
