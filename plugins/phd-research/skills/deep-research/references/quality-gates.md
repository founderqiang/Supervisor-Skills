# The six internal quality gates

Internal only; never shown to the reader. Each gate has detection questions,
a three-level verdict (CLEAR / SUSPECTED / INSUFFICIENT), and a failure
route.

## Angle (CRITICAL)

**Failure mode**: the output is a topic listing, not a survey with a
judgment.

Detection:
1. Can the survey's core judgment be stated in one sentence? Naming only the
   topic means the angle never stood up.
2. Compared with a raw search-result list, what judgment does this add? If
   the answer is "nicer formatting", it is not a survey.
3. How does the angle differ from existing surveys of the same area?

Finding a strong angle: the best angles come from unexpected convergence or
tension across independent sources: several unrelated benchmarks pointing at
one conclusion; a systematic gap between what theory worries about and what
practice struggles with; different disciplines reaching one bottleneck by
different routes. If every paper says roughly the same thing, the angle is
"consensus has formed, but remains contested under condition X".

**Route**: back to the brief; re-derive the angle.

## Coverage (MAJOR)

**Failure mode**: key works missing; conclusions built on partial evidence.

Detection:
1. Three or more works per sub-direction?
2. Does every taxonomy cell hold literature, and is each empty cell a true
   gap rather than a search miss?
3. Are the high-frequency works cited by the found papers themselves
   present?
4. Is any method school or research group entirely absent?
5. Do discarded search results point at an uncovered sub-direction?

**Route**: targeted supplementary search (back to the scouting phase).

## Citation (CRITICAL)

**Failure mode**: references that do not exist or are misquoted.

Detection:
1. Did every reference pass the verification protocol?
2. Is every specific finding quoted in the text confirmable from the paper's
   title or abstract?
3. Does any title match the argued point suspiciously perfectly?
4. For any author cited more than three times: was each entry independently
   verified?

**Route**: back to verification; invented citations are deleted on
discovery, along with every judgment that leaned on them.

## Taxonomy (MAJOR)

**Failure mode**: papers listed one by one instead of organized by theme.

Detection:
1. Are section titles theme names or paper names?
2. Are the axes mutually exclusive and collectively exhaustive?
3. Are empty cells real gaps or artifacts of the axis choice?
4. Were straddling papers identified and discussed?

**Route**: redesign the axes or regroup.

## Calibration (MAJOR)

**Failure mode**: judgment strength out of proportion to evidence strength.

Detection:
1. Does every "demonstrates / establishes" rest on multiple independent
   studies? A single study earns "suggests".
2. Any unqualified superlatives ("X is the best approach")?
3. The reverse failure too: strong convergent evidence hedged into mush
   ("may possibly suggest")?

**Route**: re-calibrate wording against the hedge ladder.

## Weaving (MAJOR)

**Failure mode**: papers summarized in isolation; no in-sentence comparison.

Detection:
1. Does every thematic paragraph have at least one sentence citing and
   comparing two or more works?
2. Any "A did X. B did Y. C did Z." runs?
3. Are contradictions presented with condition analysis, or waved off as
   "this is debated"?
4. Do the comparison tables reveal meaningful differences (columns as
   dimensions, captions stating conclusions)?

**Route**: rewrite the flagged sections against the synthesis framework.
